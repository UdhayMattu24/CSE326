Definition of Awareness:

Knowledge of existence or understanding of a situation based on information or experience.


Consumer Behaviour

Despite awareness of plastic waste issues, consumers often do not take positive action.


Types of Irresponsible Unawareness:


Recognized Unawareness:

Acknowledgment of pollution's effects but belief in insufficient information.

False Awareness:

Misplaced confidence in having complete and accurate information.

Deliberate Unawareness:

Ignoring environmental issues considered unimportant, leading to no further inquiry.

Concealed Awareness:

Withholding information due to various motivations (e.g., financial interests).

Circular Economy and Consumer Responsibility


In the circular economy, waste is a raw material.
Waste is a resource continually circulated within the economy.
It is a valuable material. The consumer is responsible
for making available correctly the recyclable waste
they produce for selective collection.


Sustainable Economic Cycle

Production:

  • Create products using sustainable materials.
  • Minimize plastic usage and focus on biodegradable alternatives.

Consumption:

  • Consumers purchase products with awareness of environmental impact.
  • Prefer brands that prioritize sustainability.

Use:

  • Use products responsibly and avoid single-use plastics.
  • Educate others about sustainable practices.

Disposal:

  • Dispose of waste properly; separate recyclables from general waste.
  • Ensure proximity to recycling bins and disposal facilities.

Recycling:

  • Recyclables are collected, sorted, and processed.
  • Close the loop by reintroducing these products to the market.



Maintaining this cycle ensures that resources are used efficiently, waste is minimized, and environmental impacts are reduced.



Consumer Roles in Reducing Plastic Pollution

  • Support plastic-free brands and contribute to municipal services.
  • Limit plastic littering while limiting bin accessibility.
  • Adopt responsible consumption while understanding pollution impacts.
  • Treat plastic as a resource.
  • Encourage a green lifestyle.

Leadership in Sustainability:


Strive to be a leader in innovative plastic recycling solutions. Focus on transforming waste into valuable resources for future use. Partner with businesses and organizations to create a circular economy.


Environmental Impact:


Commit to reducing environmental waste and promoting sustainability. Educate and empower communities about the importance of recycling.
